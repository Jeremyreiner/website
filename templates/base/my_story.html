A little about me

Growing up in Washington State, I graduated from Central Washington University with a Bachelors in Psychology. After University, I decided to move to Israel and start a new adventure. While living in Israel, I studied and eventually became fluent in Hebrew (reading / writing as well as spoken). I joined the Israeli Defense Force, and quickly moved up the ranks where I eventually finished as a Chief Sergeant in the Navy.

Outside of my military service, I began working at a local restaurant, where the owner took me under his wing. Although his whole family pitched in to help run the business, only he himself spoke English. I was certainly over my head, but I ended up staying there for quite some time. It would be that summer, almost half a year later, that I began my process of joining Garin Tzabar.

Garin tzabar is a program that assists new Israeli immigrants to serve in the Israeli Defense Forces. It was through this program that I found a new home and moved north, to a city called Regba. Upon moving I had the opportunity to meet with amazing people, who years later are still a huge part of who I am today. It was here where I was able to settle down for the majority of my time in Israel and was happy to call a home.

In my service I was put on a patrol boat guarding the northern land/sea border. My duties varied throughout my career from Deck Hand, Bosin, Mechanic, Weapons Specialist, Weapons Mechanic, and many others. I was on a team of around 12 men, who together spent the better half of two years together on a tin can in the middle of the sea. There I truly learned the value of teamwork, comradery, friendship, professionalism, and lessons that only on a boat in the middle of the sea can one learn.

Since then, I've been working on developing and honing my skills to become a Software Developer. Finishing my first bootcamp at Developers Institute, and advancing on to my next professional course strengthening my coding abilities at Harvard, taking their CS50 web development course. I'm sure that all of you reading this would be greatly advised to check out my list of projects on the link in the navbar for further details about my work!
